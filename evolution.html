Evolution of Music Consumption

The way we consume music has undergone a dramatic transformation. From vinyl records to digital streaming, each step has brought new challenges and opportunities for artists and listeners. In the 1970s, vinyl records were the dominant medium, providing a tangible connection to music. This was followed by cassette tapes and CDs, which allowed more portability and better sound quality.

As technology evolved, the introduction of digital downloads and later streaming services like Spotify and Apple Music revolutionized how we access music. Streaming provides convenience and a vast library at one's fingertips, but it has also drastically changed how artists are compensated, shifting from physical sales to per-stream royalties.
